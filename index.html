Understanding Life Expectancy

Life expectancy is a crucial indicator of a population's health and well-being. It reflects the average number of years a newborn is expected to live. In this dashboard, we explore the trends of life expectancy in Malaysia over the years, revealing insights into health disparities among different ethnic groups.

Demographic Insights

Prior to 2019, life expectancy had shown a steady increase for most ethnic groups, indicating overall improvements in healthcare and living conditions in Malaysia. However, the COVID-19 pandemic had a significant impact on life expectancy, with most groups experiencing a decline in 2020 and 2021.

Chinese males and females consistently had the highest life expectancy, with Chinese females peaking at 80.4 years in 2019 and Chinese males reaching 75.2 years the same year. However, both groups experienced a decline after the pandemic, with females dropping to 79.4 years and males to 73.5 years by 2022.

Indian males and females were the hardest hit, showing significant drops. Indian males' life expectancy fell from 68.4 years in 2019 to 66.3 years in 2022, while Indian females dropped from 76.1 years in 2019 to 73.2 years by 2022, indicating a sustained impact on this group. Similarly, Malay males and females saw declines, with male life expectancy peaking at 71.5 years in 2020–2021 before falling to 70.4 years in 2022, and female life expectancy dropping below 75 years by 2022.

Other Bumiputera males and females also faced downward trends, with males' life expectancy dipping from 72.3 years in 2019–2020 to 71.0 years by 2022, and females following a similar pattern. Conversely, non-citizens displayed more resilience, especially males who reached a high of 80.2 years in 2020, though this fell to 76.4 years in 2022, while females also showed fluctuations but maintained a comparatively higher life expectancy.

Sex and State Dynamics

Across the country, females consistently enjoyed higher life expectancies than males, a trend that holds true in every state. For instance, Kuala Lumpur's women led the pack with a remarkable 79.0 years in 2016, though this figure slipped to 76.6 years by 2022. This decline mirrors a broader national trend, where life expectancy for females decreased in many states, such as Johor, which fell from 76.9 years in 2016 to 75.4 years in 2022.

Males, too, experienced varying fortunes across the states. While Sarawak men had a relatively high life expectancy of 74.5 years in 2016, they saw a decline to 72.1 years by 2022. Meanwhile, Kedah faced significant challenges, with male life expectancy dropping from 70.2 years in 2016 to 68.6 years in 2022, signaling troubling health trends in the state.

Despite the overall higher life expectancy for females, the data reveals a concerning pattern of decline across many states. The Pahang and Kelantan regions saw similar trends, with both genders reporting reductions in life expectancy. Notably, the steady decline in states like Kelantan—where males dropped from 69.0 years in 2016 to 68.7 years in 2022—highlights the urgent need for targeted health initiatives.
